Major Historical Changes

  1. Del Mar Heights and Carmel Valley Road rerouting
  2. SR 56 Extension
  3. Pacific Highlands Ranch

Development of Carmel Valley

What we know today as Carmel Valley was developed over many years, starting in 1983 with the 'North City West' plan. The timelapse shows the change in the area from then until the modern day. Recent changes include the development of Pacific Highlands Ranch.

SR-56 Extension

SR-56 terminated after the Carmel Country Rd. exit and become Carmel Valley Road, which would continue to the east. Del Mar heights would terminate right before Carmel Valley Road.

Del Mar Heights to Carmel Valley Road Connection

With the completion of SR-56, Carmel Valley Road was shifted to cross the highway, and the previous alignment was designated Old Carmel Valley Road. Del Mar Heights was extended to meet both of them.

2003

SR-56 is routed into Carmel Valley Road, which doesn't connect to Del Mar Heights.

2004-5

The new Carmel Valley Road exit is constructed and is extended north along the original alignment and widened.

2006

The Del Mar Heights and Carmel Valley Road intersection is there as we know and love (mostly because of Trader Joe's) today.
Note: Trader Joes did not exist back then.
