Hiking

Carmel Valley offers a diverse range of hiking experiences, from easy family walks through riparian forests to moderate climbs with sweeping ocean views. The area is defined by its extensive canyon systems and protected preserves. Top Hiking Destinations in Carmel Valley

Carmel Mountain Preserve

A local favorite for its accessibility and distant ocean views. Gorgeous views of the eastern mountains and western coast. Highlights the chaparral landscapes, native plants, and well-maintained dirt and paved paths.

Los Penasquitos Canyon

Located just south and east of Carmel Valley, this preserve features a peaceful atmosphere and picturesque scenery. Los Peñasquitos Creek Waterfall is highly recommended for families and children.

Gonzales Canyon

Home to various trails including the Carmel Valley Canyon Trail and passes through scenic canyon terrain. Described as feeling like the wilderness despite being in suburban San Diego. Black Mountain Open Space Park is ocated nearby, offering more challenging terrain and panoramic vistas.

Neighborhoods

Pacific Highlands Ranch

New construction, walkable Village center. Modern, active families.

Torrey Hills

Gated, quiet, close to tech hubs. Commuter convenience, tranquility.

Carmel Country Highlands

Gated, multi-bedroom homes. Large families, school proximity.

Del Mar Mesa

Expansive lots, nature-intertwined. Privacy, safety, rural-suburban mix.

Bayshore

Modern designs, pool amenities. Range of budgets and preferences.
